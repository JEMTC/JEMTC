Project Description

Our project is to re-design the Comp1950 website to make it more user-friendly. We will create a proto-site as well as documents to support the re-design. The proto-site consists of a home page, lecture page and blog page mocked up.The mockups will give our client a sense of how the site will look. The final deliverables include: our client site, wireframes, sitemap and rationale. Our site will be HTML 5 compliant with CSS-driven navigation and design.

Goals and Process

The Comp1950 student page needs to be re-designed in order to better meet the needs of students. The client, Alistair Calder, would like to give students better access to information on the site - notes, examples, homework and other information. Our goal is to create a clean, easy-to-navigate website, where students can easily find their weekly lecture notes, homework, latest news / announcements, interesting articles and other resources. We want to present this in a current, relevant and informed way.

We went about this project in a very methodology-driven way. After thoroughly reviewing the brief, we collectively decided on a direction. We looked at the project through the view of the target audience - us, the students. We decided how we want to navigate the page, what information is useful to us, and what look / feel we wanted to portray. We first created a sitemap to decide what pages we wanted on the site. Then we created wireframes and mockups to decide where the pages, buttons and links go (the layout of the site). Then we started coding the pages, adding css and javascript. As a final step we tested the site for any broken links or errors; also made sure all pages were linked correctly. We each had separate tasks, but kept in mind the opinion of the group and direction of the brief. Using GitHub was very helpful - we all had access to the code and were able to make adjustments on an ongoing basis. We communicated by email and weekly meeting to complete the project.
